In 1968

Maharashtra State Champion in Shot-put, Javelin Throw, Discus Throw, Weight Lifting, Table Tennis, and Individual Champion.

In 1969

Gold in 50 m Freestyle Swimming at Stoke Mandeville.

In 1970

Gold in 50 m Freestyle Swimming, Silver in Javelin Throw, and Bronze in Shot-put at the 3rd Commonwealth Paraplegic Games in Edinburgh, Scotland.

In 1971

Gold in 50 m Freestyle Swimming and Silver in Javelin Throw at Stoke Mandeville, England.

In 1972

Won a Gold medal and set a new world record (37.33 seconds) in the 50 m Freestyle Swimming event at the Summer Paralympics in Heidelberg, Germany. This marked the first-ever Paralympic gold medal for India.

In 1975

Gold and another world record in Swimming at Stoke Mandeville International Paraplegic Meet, UK, along with a Silver in Shot-put.

In 1982

Gold and another world record in 50 m Swimming and a Bronze Medal in Javelin Throw at the International FESPIC Games in Hong Kong.

In 2018

Awarded the Padma Shri by the Government of India for his contributions to sports.

Murlikant Petkar

Murlikant Petkar's story is one of incredible resilience and triumph. Born on November 1, 1944, in Maharashtra, India, Petkar served in the Indian Army until he was severely injured in the 1965 India-Pakistan war, resulting in a permanent disability. Refusing to let this setback define him, he turned to sports, excelling in swimming, javelin, shot put, and table tennis. His relentless pursuit of excellence led him to the 1972 Paralympic Games in Heidelberg, Germany, where he made history as the first Indian to win a Paralympic gold medal, setting a world record in the 50-meter freestyle. Petkar's achievements and pioneering spirit continue to inspire countless athletes with disabilities, proving that true champions are defined by their response to challenges. Honored and celebrated, his legacy remains a beacon of hope and resilience.
